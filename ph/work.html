We are currently working on designing a learning management system (LMS) app that supports student-teacher communication and collaboration outside the classroom.






What is a learning management software (LMS)?

A learning management system (LMS) is a software application for the administration, documentation, tracking, reporting, and delivery of educational courses, training programs, or learning and development programs. The learning management system concept emerged directly from e-Learning.



Main Features of STUDIOUS

- Grade management
- Student roster / attendance management
- Assessment implementation
- Discussion forums
- Lesson planner
- Collaboration management
- File exchange
- Internal messaging, live chat, wikis



Challenges

When choosing an LMS, we face a question: what is better - a free solution that requires further development, or an expensive product that is ready out-of-the-box? This question reveals two general investment factors: initial price and future cost of ownership.
Regardless of the investment, a good LMS should be user-friendly, cost-effective, and suitable for the type of organization that uses it.
Studious will be developed as an LMS app to be perfectly suited for common educational goals and free up-front.
It should not come with a lot of hidden costs such as maintenance, support, etc.
Currently, the best and worst thing about Studious is its complexity - there are so many features and settings that can be a bit overwhelming for a teacher new to using an LMS.
Studious is continually being upgraded. It has immense possibilities in terms of delivering content and providing interactive materials.
Customizing the app should not be costly and time-consuming like other LMS's available on the market.
One big feature the app will eventually have is active online learning communities and collaboration activities (which is deficient in the LMS's available such as BlackBoard).

- When can we use synchronous communication and asynchronous communication in the app? What problems and benefits do they have?

- How can this app accelerate self-directed learning for high school students (especially when they and their teachers lack access to Internet connection)?

- What tasks can this app be a good supplement for?
