Challenges

Far from being the meritocracy that many people believe it to be, the education sector has become increasingly elitist in a society that is permeated with social inequalities, as various gaps continue to widen.


For many high school students who face socioeconomic or health challenges, receiving a quality education has the potential to change the trajectory of their lives. Yet, education has become more of a competition that favors those who have a headstart - with money, power, and influence.



COVID-19



COVID-19 has highlighted copious issues around the world, and education is no exception. The question of equity was brought up often: How can we ensure each student is able to receive communication and complete their work at home?

The world of education has changed drastically in the last two decades due to technology, yet it has not changed equitably. There is no simple solution for this problem. At minimum, we should ensure all students have access to computers and technology, but there are many complex issues beyond that. The reality is that all students are facing months out of school. This is a challenging situation for everyone, and one that is also likely to incerase the achievement gap between students.

Rising's team has discussed and investigated options for technology. We have severe and genuine worries about a small population of students that may not have as much access to educational tools and technology as others. We are hoping that through initiatives we can organize at home, we can help students remotely during and after the quarantine.
