Challenges

Education is the most important engine of social mobility. Yet, education as a societal resource has seldom, if ever, been distributed equally.



Race, ethnicity, disabilities and other factors remain stubborn markers of socioeconomic status in our society. Consequently, socioeconomic status encompasses income, educational attainment, and quality of life.

If we are ever to achieve opportunity equality, then we are much more likely to succeed by the broadest access to resources for all students.



The demographics alone signal immediacy.


Acting now to affect the pathways of today's high school students will change their college, career, and socioeconomic outcomes.

Given the long time horizon for demonstrable results of efforts to improve the participation and success of underrepresented minorities, we cannot delay if we want to get ahead of the workforce challenges and opportunities that are coming in the next decade.



COVID-19


The current pandemic has exacerbated many systemic inequalities that persist in our society. Low-income families and communities of color are disporporationately impacted by the COVID-19 virus and also face barriers to treatment and prevention resources. As the financial aspect of this crisis deepens, these families also struggle more than other groups with economic disenfranchisement, including job loss and resource scarcity.

As we reflect on current events, Rising is committed to inclusive excellence. We also understand that this commitment requires all of us to do more to confront injustice in the world. We know that many in our communities are struggling right now. Even if we cannot be physically together to process this loss and the challenges that seem to surround us, we believe that we can address these issues together.
