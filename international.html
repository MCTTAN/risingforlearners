Education is an essential human right. But some countries struggle to provide it.
Deep inequalities of wealth, gender, ethnicity, language, location and disabilities continue to hold back academic progress.
Some say that education needs to be reformed. However, academic progress is often blocked by political crises and conflicts, exacerbated by economic hardships and compounded by weak institutions.
This vicious cycle holds entire societies back.
